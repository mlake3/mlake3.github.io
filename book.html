Why it's My Favorite
Although I am not the biggest reader, I am particularly fond of this novel for a number of reasons. I read this book as part of a "book club" thing, and I was immediately hooked. Without spoiling too much, it tells a very compelling story about four generations of a Korean family living in Japan. It explores themes of identity, resilience, and sacrifice as they navigate discrimination, poverty, and cultural displacement throughout the 20th century. It’s a very touching and emotional book—and they even made a show based on it.
